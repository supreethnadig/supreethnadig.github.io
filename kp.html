Introduction

Located in the Western Ghats of Karnataka, India, Kumara Parvatha is the second-highest peak in Coorg district, after Tadiandamol peak.

Trek Overview: Kumara Parvatha trek is a challenging adventure that begins at Kukke Subramanya and takes you through the Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary. The trek spans 13 km one way, with an elevation gain of over 1700 meters. It’s recommended to trek over two days, camping near Bhattaramane or the Forest Office, and taking in the stunning views of the Western Ghats.

Here are key details about the trek:

  • Location: Dakshina Kannada district, Karnataka state, India
  • Distance from Bangalore: 280 km
  • Trek starting point: Kukke Subramanya Route
  • Trek difficulty level: Difficult
  • Trek distance: 13 km (one side)
  • Peak height: 1712 meters (5,600 feet)
  • Time to trek: 8-10 hours (one side)
  • Camping: Allowed near Buttaramane or near Forest Office
  • Best time to trek: October to February
  • Permission: Required, obtain from Forest Office
  • Trekking fee: ₹350
